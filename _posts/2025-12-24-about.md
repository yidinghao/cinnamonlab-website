These are questions about cinnamonlab that no one has asked me (yet), but I imagine someone will one day.

**What does "cinnamonlab" mean?**<br />
It stands for "cinnamonlab is not [Najoung's](https://najoung.kim/), [Aaron Mueller's](https://aaronmueller.github.io/), or [Naomi's](https://nsaphra.net/) lab." 

**What does the logo mean?**<br />
The logo is a picture of a cinnamon labrador retriever (a type of dog), which is apparently what you get if you Google _cinnamon lab_. The image was generated by Google Gemini. 

**Where do the pictures come from?**<br />
I uploaded various [BU stock photos](https://www.bu.edu/brand/asset-library/bu-stock-photos/) to Microsoft Copilot and gave it the following prompt: _Please convert this picture into the style of a Studio Ghibli film_.

**How do you spell "cinnamonlab"?**<br />
Either ⟨Cinnamonlab⟩ or ⟨cinnamonlab⟩. It's all one word; the first letter may be uppercase or lowercase; and all other letters must be lowercase. "Cinnamonlab" may be capitalized either like a proper noun or like a common noun.


**What are the grammatical features of _cinnamonlab_?**<br />
_Cinnamonlab_ is one lexical item, pronounced /ˈsɪnəmɨnˌlæ:b/. It is a proper noun; i.e., a determiner with no complements, whose semantic type is _e_. It has the same morphological structure as _New York City_ or _JetBlue Airways_.
